# voice-lock.md
*For Claude instances and bots writing belt.works copy.*
*Do not write public-facing copy without reading this first.*
*Last updated: 2026-04-30*

---

## The voice

Dave Kennedy register. Look it up.

Three words: **useful, utilitarian, determined.**

The chip: **money**. Not ideology. Not geography. Just money.
Write from inside that — plainly, without performing it.

---

## The lines

**Primary — use this as the CTA, the anchor, the through line:**

> We build software that works. You bring the problem.

**Secondary — supporting, not decorative:**

> Research first. Software as evidence.

These are locked. Do not rewrite them. Do not improve them.
They don't try to be memorable. They just are. That's why they work.

---

## The transaction

belt.works copy always describes a transaction, not a vision:

- you have a problem
- we build software
- it works
- bring it here

Keep that sequence visible in every piece of copy.

---

## What the voice is not

- Not inspirational
- Not Akron-against-all-odds
- Not startup-coded
- Not perfumed
- Not dressed up
- Not gatekeeping ("bring us something worth building" — wrong posture, implies judgment of the customer)
- Not pedantic
- Not aspirational

---

## Banned language

Do not use:
- innovate / innovation
- disrupt / disruption
- hustle
- scale / scalable
- ecosystem (unless technical)
- seamless
- robust
- leverage (as a verb)
- empower
- cutting-edge
- next-generation
- world-class
- passionate
- excited to announce
- game-changer
- revolutionary
- unlock (as a metaphor)
- journey
- story (as a brand concept)

---

## Tests for copy

Before publishing any copy, ask:

1. Does it describe a transaction or a feeling? → Transaction only.
2. Would Nate say this out loud? → If not, cut it.
3. Does it have perfume on it? → If yes, strip it.
4. Is there a shorter version that means the same thing? → Use that.
5. Does it make the customer sound like they need to qualify? → Wrong posture. Rewrite.

---

## What "Consumer hardware. Business results." means

This is the economic argument. It means:

- We don't run on AWS enterprise infrastructure
- We don't charge enterprise rates
- We get business-grade output anyway
- That gap is the value proposition

Use it as supporting context, not as a tagline. The primary line does the work.

---

## Akron

Context, not brand. Never the lede.

People should find out it's from Akron after they've already decided the work is good.
"Huh, interesting" — not "wow, against all odds."

Do not write the underdog story. The work is the story.
