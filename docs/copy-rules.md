# belt.works Copy Rules

Read `CONTEXT.md` and `docs/beltworks-site-direction.md` first.

## Register

Useful. Utilitarian. Determined.

State the situation. Do not perform belief.

## Lede

Research is the lede.

Software is what the research produces.

The software is evidence.

## Required Ideas

- Serious technical work can happen outside institutions.
- The work either holds up or it does not.
- Consumer hardware. Consumer spend. Business results.
- Infrastructure enables autonomous sessions.
- Autonomous sessions build products.
- Products generate evidence and revenue.
- Mailing list first. FOUNDRY intake second.

## Allowed CTA Language

- Join the mailing list.
- Bring a problem.
- Read the work.
- See what is running.
- Follow the research.

## Banned Framing

- Startup
- Agency
- Consultancy by the hour
- Akron underdog story
- Inspirational comeback
- Hustle story
- Scaling strategy
- Founder journey

## Banned Phrases

- Transform your business
- Empower innovation
- Cutting edge AI solutions
- Unlock your potential
- Next-generation platform
- Disrupt the industry
- Revolutionize your workflow
- Seamless scalable solutions
- World-class
- Game-changing

## Project Status Language

Use honest labels:

- Live
- Shippable
- Built enough to show
- Prototype
- On hold
- Needs wiring
- Waiting on client
- Waiting on hardware

Do not call unfinished work launched.

## Akron Rule

Akron is context, not brand.

Work first. Place second.

Do not write "against all odds."

## Money Rule

The constraint is money. Not ideology. Not geography.

Write plainly from inside that constraint. Do not decorate it.

## AI Rule

Do not say "AI-powered" unless the sentence explains what the system actually does.

Bad: AI-powered business solutions.

Good: Agents watch repos, dispatch tasks, collect diffs, and report back through Telegram.

## Final Check

Before publishing any public copy, ask:

1. Does this advance survival, evidence, or method?
2. Does this sound like Nate or a SaaS landing page?
3. Is it true today?
4. Would a GitHub reader respect it?
